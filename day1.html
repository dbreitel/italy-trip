1 History
Ercolano · ~20 min from airport
Herculaneum (Ercolano)

Pompeii's quieter, better-preserved sister city — and far less crowded in late June, which is a major advantage. Buried by the same Vesuvius eruption in 79 AD, Herculaneum was a wealthier seaside town, and the ash preserved wooden beams, furniture, food, and mosaics in extraordinary detail. A compact site — you can see the highlights in 1.5–2 hours without rushing.

1.5–2 hours visit Opens 9:00 AM Corso Resina, Ercolano
☀️ June heat is serious here. The site is completely open-air with almost no shade. Arrive as early as possible — aim for 9:00 opening. Bring a hat, sunscreen, and 1.5L water per person minimum. Save Pompeii for Day 2 when you have more time.
🏛️ Don't miss: The carbonised wooden furniture in the Casa del Salone Nero, the mosaic of Neptune & Amphitrite, and the boat sheds on the ancient shoreline containing skeletal remains of those who fled the eruption.
2 Scenic
Torre del Greco / Torre Annunziata · 10 min from Herculaneum
The Road Along Vesuvius

Continuing south on the SS145, the views between Torre del Greco and Castellammare di Stabia are some of the most dramatic in Italy — Vesuvius towering on your left, the turquoise Tyrrhenian Sea on your right, and ancient towns clinging to the slopes between them. Look for a layby (piazzola di sosta) and stop for a photo. The visual of the still-smouldering volcano hovering over a modern city never gets old.

10–15 min stop No entry fee — roadside view
3 Lunch
Castellammare di Stabia · 10 min south
Lunch + Supermarket Stop

Castellammare di Stabia is a genuine working-class Neapolitan seaside town — not a tourist trap. That means real prices, real food, and Italians actually eating there. Park near the lungomare (seafront promenade, Viale Europa) and find a trattoria for lunch. Afterward, the Conad or Eurospin supermarket on the main road is the perfect place to stock up before heading into the smaller villages of the peninsula.

~1 hour for lunch +20 min supermarket Viale Europa promenade
🍽️ Recommended restaurants near the waterfront: Look for any trattoria or ristorante with locals eating inside. Ask for the menù del giorno (daily menu) — usually €12–15 for a full meal with wine.

What to order:

Spaghetti alle Vongole Clams, garlic, white wine — a classic
Pizza Margherita Neapolitan-style, wood-fired
Frittura di Paranza Mixed fried small fish
Acqua frizzante Sparkling water — stay hydrated!
🛒 At the supermarket (Conad / Eurospin / Carrefour): This is your best chance to stock up at reasonable prices before reaching the tourist peninsula.

Essential shopping list:

Still & sparkling water (2L+ per person)
Sunscreen (SPF50 minimum)
Snacks: fruit, nuts, crackers

Fresh mozzarella & local cheese

Bread or focaccia
Limoncello (it's cheaper here!)
4 Viewpoint
Sorrentine Peninsula · ~40 min from Castellammare
Punta della Campanella Viewpoint

As you wind through the hills above Massa Lubrense on the SP9 road, you'll pass viewpoints offering jaw-dropping panoramas of the Tyrrhenian Sea and the island of Capri floating in the distance. Punta della Campanella is the southernmost tip of the peninsula — a rocky cape facing Capri just 5km away. You don't need to hike all the way down (leave that for another day), but any of the miradors along the ridge road are spectacular for a first sunset view of the area.

20–30 min stop Best in late afternoon light Free — roadside viewpoint
📸 Golden hour photo tip: The drive along the SP9 from Termini toward Massa Lubrense in late afternoon (around 6–7pm in June) offers extraordinary light over Capri and the bay — genuinely one of the most photographed views in Italy.
🏨 Hotel
Via delle Torre 7, Massa Lubrense · 15 min from viewpoint
Arrival at Your Hotel

Massa Lubrense is a peaceful, genuine Sorrentine village — a world away from tourist-heavy Sorrento just a few kilometres away. The locals actually live here. After settling in, head out on foot to explore the village centro and find a restaurant for dinner. Via Nastro d'Oro and the piazza near the Duomo di Santa Maria delle Grazie have a handful of excellent local spots.

Aim to arrive by 6–7pm Dinner: walk the village
🚗 Park and stay parked. Once at the hotel, leave the car for the night. Do not move it — parking in Massa Lubrense is limited and the roads at night are very narrow. Walk or take a taxi for dinner.

Driving Tips for Day 1
🚦 ZTL Zones

Red circles with "ZTL" on signs mean No Entry for non-resident vehicles. Cameras are everywhere. Fines of €80–150 arrive by post. Always check Waze settings to avoid these areas.

🅿️ Parking your rental

Rental cars are prime targets for break-ins throughout Campania. Never leave anything visible in the car — bags, cameras, even charging cables. Use hotel parking when possible.

🛣️ Road widths on the peninsula

The SP9 and smaller roads near Massa Lubrense are very narrow — one lane shared in both directions. Pull into a passing space when meeting oncoming traffic. Do not rush. Locals will honk; ignore it.

🌡️ Late June traffic

Expect x1.5 to x2 the navigation time estimate on coastal roads in late June — especially on weekends. The A3 motorway is reliable; everything else may crawl. Plan for it.

🇮🇹 Local driving style

Southern Italian drivers are assertive. Overtaking, tailgating, and horn use are all normal. Stay calm, don't respond aggressively, and you'll be fine. Deep breaths.

⛽ Fuel

Fill up in Castellammare di Stabia before entering the Sorrentine Peninsula — fuel stations become sparse and expensive further along. Always keep at least half a tank.
