Your Day, Stop by Stop
8:00 AM · Departure

Leave Massa Lubrense — head east on SS145

Start early to beat the worst of the coastal traffic. The drive from Massa Lubrense to Positano takes about 25–30 minutes via Sorrento on a good morning.

⚠️ Parking tip: Do NOT try to drive into Positano's centre. Park at the large pay car park on the upper road (Viale Pasitea) as you arrive — they fill up fast. The walk downhill into town is pleasant and easy.
8:30–10:30 AM · First Stop

🌸 Positano

One of the most beautiful villages in all of Italy. Pastel-coloured houses tumble down the cliffs to a turquoise sea. Stroll Viale Pasitea and Via Cristoforo Colombo, peek inside the lovely Chiesa di Santa Maria Assunta (famous for its majolica-tiled dome), and enjoy a morning coffee with that view. The lower beach, Spiaggia Grande, is photogenic even if you're not swimming.

💡 For over-50s: The descent into town involves many steps. The upper road area has plenty of beautiful viewpoints if anyone prefers to avoid the steep climb back up.
10:30 AM · Drive

Continue east on SS163 through Praiano

Drive slowly — the stretch of road through Praiano hugs the cliff edge and offers some of the most photographed views on the entire coast. Pull over at any of the small laybys for photos. Praiano itself is quieter and less touristy than its neighbours, worth a short stretch of the legs.

11:00–11:45 AM · Second Stop

🟢 Grotta dello Smeraldo — The Emerald Cave

One of Italy's most beautiful sea caves. The water glows an extraordinary emerald green from refracted light. You descend by lift or steps from the road, then take a short boat ride inside. Not strenuous at all and absolutely magical. Book tickets at the entrance.

12:00–2:30 PM · Third Stop

⛵ Amalfi Town

Park in the large seafront car park (expensive but the only sensible option — pre-book if possible). Walk up to the magnificent Cattedrale di Sant'Andrea — the striped Arab-Norman façade is one of the great sights of southern Italy. Duck into the cloister (Chiostro del Paradiso). Explore the small lanes. Have a long, relaxed lunch at one of the restaurants along Piazza Duomo or a side street — try fresh pasta with local clams or the local lemon desserts.

🍋 Must-try: Delizia al limone — a cream-filled lemon sponge that is the signature dessert of the Amalfi Coast.
2:30 PM · Drive uphill (~20 min)

🍽️ Head up to Ravello

A narrow winding road takes you 350 metres above sea level to one of the most serene and beautiful hill towns in Italy. The drive itself is dramatic and rewarding.

3:00–5:30 PM · Final Stop

🎵 Ravello — The Balcony of the Amalfi Coast

Wagner composed here. Escher painted here. Artists and composers came to Ravello for its extraordinary panoramas over the coastline. Visit Villa Rufolo (lovely gardens, 10th-century towers) and stroll to the famous Terrazza dell'Infinito at Villa Cimbrone — the view from that terrace is arguably the finest on the entire coast. The village is calm, elegant and perfect for a slow afternoon aperitivo.

💡 Tip: Ravello is ZTL-free for tourists arriving at the car parks near the entrance. Walk in from there — the village is small and entirely walkable.
🚗 Driving the Amalfi Coast with a car: The SS163 is genuinely narrow in places. Drive slowly and be patient — locals know these roads and will squeeze past confidently. ZTL (restricted traffic zones) are enforced by cameras in several towns including Amalfi itself. Use Waze or Google Maps and select "avoid toll roads/ZTL" in settings. Never enter a ZTL sign — fines arrive home by post weeks later.
Return to Massa Lubrense: From Ravello retrace to Amalfi, then continue west on SS163 all the way back through Positano to Sorrento and then south to Massa Lubrense. Or go east through Salerno and loop back on the A3 motorway — faster but less scenic. Allow 1.5–2 hrs for the return.

8:00 AM · Departure

Drive Massa Lubrense → Pompeii (~35 min)

Head north on the SS145 toward Sorrento, then pick up the SS18 east toward Pompeii. The drive is straightforward. Aim to arrive before 9am when the site opens — it gets very crowded and hot by mid-morning.

🎟️ Essential: Book your Pompeii tickets online in advance at pompeiitickets.com. On-site queues can be 45–90 minutes long. Skip-the-line entry is worth every cent.
9:00 AM–1:00 PM · Main Event

🌋 Pompeii Archaeological Site

One of the most remarkable places on earth — an entire Roman city flash-frozen by Vesuvius in 79 AD. Plan to spend a good 3–4 hours. The western section holds the highlights: the Forum, the Suburban Baths (with their famous erotic frescoes), the House of the Faun with its magnificent mosaic, the Via dell'Abbondanza (the main shopping street), and the haunting plaster casts of victims. The scale of the site is astonishing — it genuinely feels like a city, not ruins.

👟 Footwear: The cobbled streets are uneven ancient basalt. Comfortable closed shoes with grip are essential — this is not a sandals day. Bring water, sunscreen and a hat. There is almost no shade inside the site.
1:00–2:00 PM · Lunch

🍕 Lunch in or near Pompeii

There are restaurants right outside the main entrance (Porta Marina exit). Or drive 10 minutes back toward Torre Annunziata for a more local experience. This is the region where pizza was born — a Neapolitan-style Margherita or Marinara here is as authentic as it gets.

2:30 PM · Drive

Drive to Sorrento (~25 min)

Head back southwest on the SS18 and then the SS145. Park in one of the large car parks at the edge of Sorrento's centre — the Piazza Lauro or the multi-storey near Via degli Aranci. Sorrento's historic centre is pedestrianised.

3:00–6:30 PM · Afternoon

🍋 Sorrento — The Pearl of the Peninsula

Sorrento is elegant, lively and very walkable. Start at Piazza Tasso, the beating heart of the town, ringed with cafes and overlooking the cliffs. Stroll Corso Italia for shopping. Visit the Vallone dei Mulini — a surreal abandoned medieval mill in a natural gorge, glimpsed from a street-level viewpoint. Walk to the Terrazza di Villa Comunale for the classic Sorrento cliff-top view over the Bay of Naples with Vesuvius in the background. The Museo Correale has lovely local art and antiquities. End the afternoon with a Limoncello tasting — this is where it was invented.

🍸 Aperitivo time: Sorrento's bars do a proper Italian aperitivo hour from about 6pm. Grab a table on Piazza Tasso and watch the evening passeggiata begin — one of the great pleasures of southern Italian life.
7:00 PM · Return

Drive back to Massa Lubrense (~20 min)

Head south on the SS145. A quick and easy return — or consider staying in Sorrento for dinner before heading back, as the town has excellent restaurants open until late.

💡 Optional add-on: If the group has energy and interest, the Herculaneum ruins (Ercolano) are on the way between Naples and Pompeii. Smaller, better-preserved, and far less crowded than Pompeii — a real hidden gem. It would make for an earlier start and a very full day, but it's magnificent.
☀️ Heat warning: Pompeii in summer can be brutal — exposed ancient cobblestones radiate heat and there's minimal shade. Start as early as possible (gates open at 9am), rest in the shade of the Forum colonnade during the hottest hour, and carry at least 1.5L of water per person. The site's bar/café near the exit is a welcome refuge.

7:30 AM · Departure

Drive to Sorrento · Park the car for the day

Drive to Sorrento (about 20 minutes from Massa Lubrense) and park in one of the large car parks near the port — Piazza Lauro multi-storey is convenient. Today the car stays behind. Leave it and walk 5 minutes down to the port (Porto di Sorrento).

🎟️ Tip: Buy your return ferry tickets to Capri in advance online (Alilauro, SNAV, or Caremar) or first thing at the port kiosk. High-speed hydrofoils take about 20 minutes; slower ferries take 40 min but are more comfortable. First departures are around 7:30–8:00 AM.
8:30 AM · Arrive at Marina Grande

⚓ Capri — First Impressions

The hydrofoil docks at Marina Grande, Capri's main harbour. It's immediately striking — colourful houses rise steeply above a glittering bay dotted with yachts. From here, take the funicular up to Capri Town (a 5-minute ride with spectacular views) or take a taxi/bus to wherever you're headed first.

9:00–10:30 AM · First Priority

🟦 The Blue Grotto (Grotta Azzurra)

This is the island's crown jewel — a sea cave where refracted sunlight turns the water an unearthly, luminous blue. Go first thing in the morning: the cave can close in rough seas, and by mid-morning queues for the rowing boats are very long. From Marina Grande, take a small motorboat around the island's western cape (included in the entrance price ticket, about €15pp for the cave entry on top of the boat fare). You lie flat in a tiny rowing boat to enter through a low arch into the glowing interior. Magical.

⚠️ Important: The Blue Grotto closes if the sea is rough (happens even on sunny days). Check conditions on the day. If closed, an alternative is a boat tour around the island — the Faraglioni rock stacks and sea arches are equally spectacular from the water.
11:00 AM–1:00 PM

🌿 Anacapri & Monte Solaro Chairlift

Take the bus from Marina Grande (or Capri Town) up to Anacapri, the quieter, higher village on the island. From Anacapri, the open chairlift (seggiovia) ascends to Monte Solaro — the island's summit at 589 metres. The 12-minute ride up is gentle and offers extraordinary views over the Bay of Naples, the Amalfi Coast, and on clear days even the Sicilian mountains. The summit has a small bar. The descent by chairlift is even better — you face outward over the sea.

1:00–2:30 PM · Lunch

🍝 Lunch in Anacapri or Capri Town

Anacapri has more authentic, less tourist-trap restaurants than Capri Town. Try Trattoria Il Solitario (a local favourite hidden in a garden) or any of the small places on Via Giuseppe Orlandi. Expect fresh grilled fish, local pasta, and a glass of chilled Falanghina white wine.

💰 Budget note: Capri is expensive — this is one of Italy's most exclusive islands. Lunch for four in a mid-range restaurant will be €60–100+. It's worth it for the setting, but there are no bargains here.
3:00–6:00 PM · Afternoon

🛍️ Capri Town · Passeggiata · Farewell Aperitivo

Descend to Capri Town and spend the afternoon strolling the famous Piazzetta — the tiny, glamorous main square where the world's rich and beautiful have been seen for a century. Window-shop along Via Camerelle (Gucci, Prada, Hermès all have boutiques here). Walk out to the Augustus Gardens (Giardini di Augusto) for a panoramic view of the Faraglioni rocks. End with an aperitivo at a bar on the Piazzetta — the most expensive drinks of your trip, and absolutely worth it.

6:30–7:00 PM · Return Ferry

⛴️ Ferry back to Sorrento

Last hydrofoils from Capri to Sorrento run until around 8:00–9:00 PM (check the current timetable). A 20-minute crossing as the light turns golden over the Bay of Naples. Pick up your car and drive back to Massa Lubrense for a late dinner.

🌅 Evening option: Rather than rushing back, have dinner in Sorrento before driving home. The town is lovely in the evening and the 20-minute drive back to Massa Lubrense is easy at night.
🚗 No car on Capri: Private cars are essentially banned on Capri (only residents can drive). This is not a problem — the island has buses, taxis, and funiculars, and everything is manageable on foot, especially for four fit adults. Taxis are available but expensive; buses are cheap and frequent.
📅 Best day for Capri: Avoid Saturdays and Italian public holidays when the island is overwhelmed with day-trippers. A Tuesday or Wednesday is ideal. Go early in the morning and you'll have a much more pleasant experience than those arriving by the noon rush boats.
